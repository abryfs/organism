# Demand Signal Research

## Purpose

Find evidence that real people want what you're building. Not hypothetical demand — actual signals from forums, reviews, social media, search trends, and competitor traction.

## Process

### Step 1: Define the Question

What are we validating? Be specific:

- "Do solopreneurs in the Philippines need automated Lazada order monitoring?"
- "Would food stall owners pay for AI-generated social media posts?"
- "Is 'AI employee' positioning resonating better than 'AI assistant'?"

### Step 2: Research Sources

Use WebSearch and WebFetch to investigate:

**Forum & Community Signals**
- Reddit threads about the problem (r/entrepreneur, r/smallbusiness, r/Philippines)
- Facebook groups where the target users hang out
- Quora questions about the problem
- Twitter/X conversations about the pain

Capture: exact complaints, upvote counts, frequency of mentions.

**Review Mining**
- App Store / Play Store reviews of competitors
- G2/Capterra reviews of adjacent tools
- Amazon reviews of books about the problem (reveals pain points)

Capture: 1-star reviews (what's broken), 5-star reviews (what delights), feature requests.

**Search Demand**
- Google Trends for problem-related keywords
- "How to [solve this problem]" search volume
- Related searches that reveal adjacent needs

Capture: search volume trends, geographic concentration, seasonal patterns.

**Competitor Traction**
- Competitor pricing (what the market bears)
- Competitor user counts (if public)
- Competitor funding (market confidence)
- Competitor reviews (what users love/hate)

Capture: pricing anchors, positioning language, gaps in their offering.

**Direct Demand Signals**
- Waitlist signups (if applicable)
- Inbound inquiries
- Social media DMs asking "when is this available?"

### Step 3: Synthesize

```markdown
# Demand Signal Report: [Question]
Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]

## Signal Strength: [Strong / Moderate / Weak / None]

## Evidence

### People talking about this problem
- [Source]: "[exact quote]" — [upvotes/engagement]
- [Source]: "[exact quote]" — [upvotes/engagement]
- [count] mentions found across [sources searched]

### What they're doing today
- [Current solution]: [adoption level]
- [Current solution]: [adoption level]
- Gap: [what no current solution provides]

### Willingness to pay
- Competitors charge: [$X-Y/mo range]
- Users say: "[quote about pricing]"
- Price sensitivity: [high/medium/low]

### Market size indicators
- [Relevant stat about market size]
- [Relevant stat about growth]

## What This Means for Us
- Validates: [what this confirms about our direction]
- Challenges: [what this questions]
- Opportunity: [gap we could fill that nobody else is]

## Recommended Action
- [Build / Pivot / Investigate further / Kill]
- Reasoning: [why, based on evidence above]
```

Output to `research/demand-[topic]-YYYY-MM-DD.md`

### Step 4: Connect to North Star

If findings change our understanding:
- Update NORTH-STAR.md personas with real market language
- Adjust positioning based on how users describe their pain
- Flag features that have zero demand evidence

## Research Quality Rules

- **Quote real people, not summaries.** "I waste 4 hours every week copy-pasting from Lazada to my spreadsheet" beats "Users want automation."
- **Count signals.** "5 Reddit threads with 200+ upvotes about this problem" is stronger than "People discuss this online."
- **Note absence of signal.** Finding nothing is data. "Searched 12 forums, found 0 mentions" means demand may not exist.
- **Separate demand from interest.** "That's a cool idea" (interest, worthless) vs "I would pay for this today" (demand, valuable).
- **Time-bound your research.** Spend 30 minutes max. If you can't find demand signals in 30 minutes of searching, that IS the signal.
- **Update, don't hoard.** Research that sits in a file is useless. Connect every finding to a product decision.
